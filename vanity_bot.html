About Vanity

Vanity has been a passion project since about 2017.
The concept: "How ridiculous can a discord bot be?"

EDIT 09/13/2024

Vanity has been upgraded to its next major version, Garlic, continuing the playful tradition of naming major rewrites after vegetables. This update introduces a new model called DarkIdol, powered by Ollama, which offers a faster backend for enhanced processing capabilities.

Main Features

Vanity's current version utilizes an advanced, uncensored language model (LLM) to generate responses, ensuring a nuanced and accurate communication style. Integrated with a sophisticated voice model, this iteration goes beyond textual interaction by audibly delivering responses on Discord. This dual capability not only enhances engagement but also adds a dynamic layer to the platform's functionality. Users benefit from a more immersive experience where spoken responses complement textual ones, enriching the overall interaction and usability of Vanity significantly.

Minecraft History

In historical versions, Vanity had the capability to connect to any vanilla Minecraft server using mineflayer.js and interact with players using commands sent to her in discord. Plans were in place to advance its functionality to procedurally understand player actions and offer assistance. However, the Minecraft 1.13 Flattening update rendered the bot incompatible, leading to its functionality breaking down. With the popularity of the new update among players who were testing the bot, I redirected development efforts towards implementing different features. Unfortunately, this update, with its promising advancements, became a lost chapter in Vanity's development history.

Until the recent surge in AI development brought new potential to this part of the project, there was little progress. However, a new tool, mindcraft.js, offers a fresh approach to using Mineflayer. Although I have explored this application, it currently does not meet the community's needs. Specifically, the bot cannot utilize the Minecraft Forge Protocol for connections, which is a critical requirement for our purposes. Additionally, the bot would rely on ChatGPT+ but I wanted the project to remain local, avoiding reliance on external services to maintain control and privacy. Consequently, this update still does not bring Vanity back into the game.

Chat Features

In Vanity's earlier versions, I incorporated text recognition using regular expressions (regex) to detect cussing. When Vanity identified inappropriate language, she would humorously overreact by calling the user names, all in good fun. This playful, sassy personality significantly increased user engagement, making interactions with Vanity more entertaining and memorable. These humorous responses and interactions were instrumental in setting up a personality for later AI development, highlighting the potential of AI to create dynamic and engaging user experiences.

I tried various hype models, including Llama3 8B and Lexi Llama3 (uncensored version), but they either didn't work, were incompatible with my library, or were excessively censored. After several unsuccessful attempts with these models, I decided to revisit Nous Hermes. Given the passage of time, I discovered a new model: Nous Hermes 2 Solar. This model offered a fresh opportunity to enhance Vanity's capabilities while preserving her unique, engaging personality.

Speech Synthesis

While not the cleanest implementation, I integrated speech capability into Vanity using OpenVoice, a versatile voice cloning tool. This addition, despite lacking a detailed history, involved experimenting with approximately a dozen different voices. Despite its relatively brief implementation period, I believe it's significant enough to warrant discussion. This enhancement allowed Vanity to audibly communicate responses, adding a new dimension to user interactions. While the journey with OpenVoice is still unfolding, its impact on Vanity's functionality and user experience is noteworthy, demonstrating the ongoing evolution of AI-driven interactions.
